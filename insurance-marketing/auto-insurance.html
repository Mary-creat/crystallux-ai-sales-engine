Three core coverages

What auto insurance actually covers

Liability

Mandatory in every province. Pays for damage and injury you cause to others. Most provinces require $200K minimum; we recommend $1M–$2M because lawsuits don't cap at the minimum.

Collision

Pays to repair your vehicle after a collision regardless of fault. Optional but typically required by lenders/leases. Choose a deductible ($500/$1,000/$2,500), higher deductible = lower premium.

Comprehensive

Covers theft, vandalism, hail, fire, falling objects, wildlife collisions, glass breakage. Optional but cheap relative to the loss it covers.

Optional cover that matters

Endorsements worth considering

OPCF 20a / accident benefits topup

The default accident-benefits limits in most provinces are low. Topping up to $1M medical/rehab and $1,000/week income replacement closes a serious gap for a small premium.

OPCF 27 / non-owned auto

Cover when you drive a rental or borrowed car. Cheaper than buying it at the rental counter and applies to every rental for the year.

OPCF 43 / waiver of depreciation

Pays for a new vehicle if yours is written off within the first 24–48 months. Especially valuable if you bought rather than leased.

Loss of use

Pays for a rental while your vehicle is being repaired or replaced. Capped at $50–$75/day for 30 days, typically.

Discounts we apply

Reasons your premium might drop

Multi-line

Bundle auto + home / tenant for typically 5–15% off both.

Telematics / usage

Insurers like Intact, Aviva, and CAA offer 5–25% savings for safer drivers willing to share telematics data.

Winter tires

Mandatory 5%+ discount in Ontario for verified winter tires Nov 1 – Apr 1.

Anti-theft devices

Approved tracking devices (Tag, LoJack) can yield 5–25% comprehensive discount.

Group / professional

Alumni, professional, and union groups often unlock 5–15% discounts.

Loyalty + clean record

5+ years claim-free typically earns the lowest tier. Some carriers offer accident forgiveness.
