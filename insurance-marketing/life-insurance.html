Three product types

Term, whole, or universal?

Term life

Fixed coverage for a set period (10, 20, or 30 years). Cheapest cover per dollar of protection. Ideal for covering a mortgage or income while children are dependants.

  • Lowest premium for the most cover
  • Renewable / convertible options
  • Coverage typically ends at 65–80

Whole life

Permanent coverage with a guaranteed cash value that grows over time. Higher premium but lifelong cover. Used for estate equalisation, charitable giving, business succession.

  • Coverage for life
  • Tax-advantaged cash value
  • Premiums level for life or limited-pay

Universal life

Permanent cover with investment flexibility. You direct the investment component within the policy. Best for sophisticated buyers or business owners optimising tax.

  • Investment flexibility
  • Adjustable premium / cover
  • Tax-sheltered growth

Should I have life insurance?

A 30-second decision tree

You probably need life insurance if…

  • Someone depends on your income (spouse, children, parents)
  • You have a mortgage you couldn't pay off from savings
  • You co-signed loans your family would inherit
  • You're a business owner with key-person or buy-sell exposure
  • You want to leave a legacy / equalise an estate / fund a charity

You may not need it (yet) if…

  • You're single, no dependants, no debt
  • Your estate already covers final expenses and tax
  • You have sufficient self-insured assets (HNW)

Even then, applying young locks in low rates while you're insurable. Many people buy a small term policy in their 20s and convert later.
