Why this matters

Provincial health doesn't cover visitors.

The real cost of an ER visit in Canada

For a non-resident visitor, a single trip to a Canadian emergency room can run $3,000–$8,000. An overnight hospital stay averages $5,000–$12,000 per night. Surgery for a serious medical event (heart attack, stroke, broken hip) often exceeds $50,000–$150,000. None of this is covered by OHIP or any other provincial plan for someone here on a visitor visa.

What Super Visa insurance covers

  • Emergency hospital + physician costs
  • Prescription drugs during emergencies
  • Diagnostic services (X-ray, MRI, blood work)
  • Emergency dental
  • Ambulance + medical evacuation home if needed
  • Repatriation in worst-case scenarios
Super Visa requirements

What IRCC asks for, what we provide

Minimum $100,000 coverage

Every Super Visa application requires a Canadian insurer's policy with at least $100,000 in medical coverage. Our policies meet or exceed this. Higher-limit options ($150K, $200K) also available.

Valid for 1 year minimum

The policy must cover at least 365 days from entry. We issue 1-year policies as standard; longer multi-year options available where the carrier permits.

Canadian insurer

Coverage must come from a Canadian insurance company. We quote across multiple Canadian carriers including Manulife, Sun Life, GMS, and others.

Pre-existing conditions

Honest disclosure protects your claim

Important for older visitors: Super Visa insurance has strict pre-existing-condition rules. Most policies require a "stability period", typically 90 or 180 days, during which the condition must be stable (no new symptoms, no medication changes, no diagnostic tests). Honest disclosure during the application is the only way to ensure a claim is paid. Your LLQP-licensed Crystallux advisor walks through every question with you in plain language before you buy.
