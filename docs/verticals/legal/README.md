## Why this vertical is INACTIVE

Not a fit problem — a compliance problem. Law society advertising rules across Canadian provinces (LSO Ontario Rule 4.2, Law Society of BC Rule 4, Quebec Barreau, etc.) require every outreach touch to be reviewed against jurisdiction-specific restrictions: no outcome claims, no comparative advertising, no client testimonials referencing outcomes. Activating this vertical requires a law-society-practising advising lawyer to pre-approve every template. Defer until a founder legal client signs who can help establish the compliant template bank.

## Three personas (for when ready to activate)

1. **Solo family / immigration / real estate lawyer** — 1-3 years solo, $300-600K billings, unpredictable retainer flow. High-emotion clients, short cycles.
2. **Boutique commercial litigation or employment firm (2-8 lawyers)** — $1-4M revenue, partners with distinct books, no shared marketing. Highest fit for Crystallux once activated.
3. **Fractional GC / commercial contract counsel** — longer cycle, higher LTV. B2B corporate audience, respond to LinkedIn + email sequences.

## Compliance (the activation blocker)

- **Law Society of Ontario (LSO) Rule 4.2** and equivalents in every province.
- **No outcome claims** ("we win cases", "we get your kids back").
- **No comparative advertising** ("better than [other firm]").
- **No client testimonials referencing outcomes** without written consent on file.
- **Avvo-style ratings** not permitted in some provinces.
- PIPEDA for prospective-client intake data.

**Activation requirement:** a partnering lawyer or external counsel pre-approves every outreach template per province of outreach. Expect 2-3 week compliance onboarding per new province.

## First-client timeline (when activated)

21-45 business days. Longest cycle in the vertical catalog. Lawyers evaluate every vendor through compliance lens; expect 2-3 rounds of template edits before go-live.

## Blockers to activation

1. Need a legal-industry advising lawyer willing to sign off on templates.
2. Per-province copy variants (Ontario / BC / Alberta / Quebec minimum).
3. Slower sales cycle means higher CAC; may need to offer longer free trial or ROI guarantee to win first case study client.
