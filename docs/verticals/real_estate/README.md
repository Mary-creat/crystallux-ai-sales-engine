## Why this vertical ranks #2

Real estate is volume + urgency. Agents close decisions fast because every month without listings is a month of zero commission. Canadian market: ~160,000 active agents, average agent does 4 deals/year, top 10% do 40+. Crystallux moves an average agent into the top quartile. Market is price-conscious but the ROI math on one listing (12-18K commission) covers 10 months of platform fees.

## Three personas

1. **Mid-career solo agent (5-15 years)** — 8-20 deals/yr, hits 40% commission splits at a franchise brokerage (Royal LePage, Re/Max, Century 21), wants to break to team-lead status. Willing to spend on predictable listings.
2. **Team lead (3-10 agents)** — runs a micro-team, each agent with their own CRM, coordination overhead huge. Wants shared top-of-funnel, per-agent lead distribution.
3. **New agent (0-3 years)** — eager but underfunded. Will sign for $997/mo variant if we offer one; strong referral generator but churn-prone when winter hits.

## Pain signals (from migration)

1. Zillow and Realtor.ca eating referral flow
2. FSBO lists picked over within 24 hours
3. Open house traffic dropping year-over-year
4. Seller leads going cold before the listing appointment
5. Buyer leads ghosting after one showing
6. Paying 40+ percent referral fees to lead aggregators
7. Social media content treadmill with no pipeline ROI
8. Competing with 10 agents for every expired listing
9. New-agent competition undercutting commission rates
10. Winter and spring market swings creating income gaps
11. Tech stack bloat: CRM, IDX, drip, no single system
12. Sphere-of-influence mining losing effectiveness year by year

## Compliance

CASL + PIPEDA + provincial real estate council rules (RECO in Ontario, CREA nationally, BCREA in BC). Avoid outcome claims ("we'll sell your house fast"), avoid comparison to other agents. DNCL check before voice to homeowners.

## First-client timeline

3-7 business days. Real estate agents sign fast when the case math is clear. First outreach within 48h of contract.
