# Construction & General Contracting — Vertical Profile

**Rank:** #3 (active)
**Founding price:** $1,497/mo CAD · **Retail:** $2,497/mo · **Upsell:** $3,497/mo (Construction Growth)
**Target outcome:** 20 qualified homeowner leads/month actively planning renovations
**Guarantee:** 10 qualified leads in first 30 days or month free.

## Why this vertical ranks #3

Deal size is high (average reno project $40-120K), making the platform math trivial to sell. Contractors are skeptical of tech vendors but respond to peer-advisor framing. Sales cycle is 7-14 days — slower than consulting/real_estate because the operator is usually on a jobsite during business hours.

## Three personas

1. **Residential reno GC (3-20 employees)** — runs kitchens, basements, additions. $1-5M revenue. Strong hands, weak marketing. Relies on HomeStars + referrals. Open to a system that books quotes automatically.
2. **Commercial GC** — focuses on tenant buildouts, office renos, warehouse work. Longer sales cycles (60-90 days) but bigger contracts. Best reached via LinkedIn + email.
3. **Specialty trade (roofing, HVAC, electrical, plumbing)** — sub-$2M revenue but high volume, 50+ jobs/yr. Price-sensitive on platform fee; may prefer the $997 tier if we build one.

## Pain signals (from migration)

1. Weeks of downtime between projects
2. Paying Google Ads $40-80 per lead that never convert
3. Losing bids to bigger firms with marketing budgets
4. Relying on word-of-mouth and referrals with no system
5. Crew sitting idle while waiting for next project
6. HomeStars and Houzz fees eating into profit margins
7. Bidding against unlicensed contractors
8. Seasonal gaps, winter slowdowns, spring scramble
9. Spending evenings quoting instead of running the business
10. Missed callbacks because no one is in the office
11. Clients ghosting after the quote
12. Cannot compete with large firms on SEO

## Compliance

CASL + CRTC DNCL for voice outreach. Target licensed contractors only (provincial licensing checks — MCCSS in Ontario, BC Housing in BC, etc.). WSIB clearance recommended for client-side credibility.

## First-client timeline

7-14 business days. Operators often out of office during business hours; expect 2-3 scheduling cycles before first demo. First outreach can send within 72h of contract.

## Blockers

- Operator availability — jobsite owners are hard to reach during 9-5. Evening/weekend voice outreach may need DNCL carve-outs.
- Commercial segment requires longer consultative cycle; start with residential-focused clients.
