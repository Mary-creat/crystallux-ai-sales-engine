## Why this vertical ranks #4

Dental practices have strong retention economics — one new patient is worth $1,500-3,500 LTV on routine + cosmetic. Owners are sophisticated buyers who understand chair-hour economics. Ranks below the top 3 because the regulatory advertising environment (RCDSO Ontario, Alberta Dental Association, etc.) requires copy compliance review per province before any outreach goes live.

## Three personas

1. **Solo practice owner-operator** — 1-2 operatories, running the practice day-to-day, books their own recalls. Strong clinical reputation, weak marketing. Highest-fit Crystallux buyer.
2. **Multi-location practice owner** — 3-8 locations, each with its own front desk. Needs centralised lead pipeline routed per location. Pricing pressure but values efficiency.
3. **Cosmetic / specialty practice (ortho, endo, perio)** — smaller patient pool, higher per-case value ($5K-15K). Happy to pay premium if pipeline produces right-fit cases.

## Compliance

CASL + PIPEDA + provincial regulatory body advertising rules. RCDSO Ontario is strictest: no outcome claims, no diagnostic claims, no comparative claims, no testimonials referencing specific treatments. Each province has own rules — copy must be reviewed per province before first send.

## First-client timeline

10-21 business days. Dentists are in clinic 4-5 days/week; demo scheduling is a bottleneck. Front-desk must be trained to route inbound lead calls to the new system during week 1.

## Blockers

- Regulatory copy review per province adds 3-5 days before activation.
- RCDSO rules require avoiding outcome language ("whiter teeth", "pain-free"); outreach templates need dental-specific copy review.
- Front desk change-management is real — if they don't cooperate, consults don't get booked even when leads come in.
