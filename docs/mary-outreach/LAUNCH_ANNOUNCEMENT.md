# Launch Announcement — Crystallux Goes Public

**Purpose:** content for the public launch of Crystallux. One coordinated day, multiple channels, designed to produce the maximum warm-pipeline bump without feeling like PR noise.

**Launch day target:** pick a Tuesday or Wednesday, mid-month, not adjacent to a holiday. Tuesday 9am Eastern for best LinkedIn + newsletter open rates.

**Expected outcomes:**
- 20-40 warm inbound enquiries within 72 hours
- 5-10 booked demos from Mary's network alone
- 3-5 signed clients within 14 days from launch-day traffic
- Compounding LinkedIn visibility for 30-60 days

---

## Launch day roll-out (sequence)

**Tuesday 9:00am ET** — LinkedIn founder-voice post (Mary's personal profile)

**Tuesday 10:00am ET** — Email to Mary's personal and professional network (BCC, under 50 recipients per batch)

**Tuesday 12:00pm ET** — Crystallux LinkedIn company page launch post + announcement

**Tuesday 2:00pm ET** — Begin tagging 10 selected individuals for amplification (friendly asks only)

**Wednesday-Friday** — Respond to every comment within 2-4 hours; follow up on every DM within 24 hours

**Week 2** — Press angle pitch to 3-5 Canadian tech / SMB journalists (if pipeline allows)

## LinkedIn post — Mary's personal profile (founder voice)

**Tone:** personal, specific, zero hype. No emojis. No "excited to announce". Genuine-operator voice.

**Length:** ~250-300 words. LinkedIn truncates after the first 3-4 lines; front-load the hook.

```
Six months ago I set out to solve a problem I kept hearing from
every Canadian broker, contractor, and consultant in my network:

"The sales pipeline only moves when I personally move it."

Weekends disappear into proposals. Bizdev eats the hours that
should go to delivery. And the outreach that actually works —
peer-to-peer, specific, human — doesn't scale past 10 leads a
week.

Today I'm launching Crystallux.

Crystallux is an AI sales engine built for Canadian operators.
Apollo-powered prospect sourcing. Claude-authored outreach tuned
to your vertical. Multi-channel delivery across email, LinkedIn,
SMS, voice, and video. Qualified meetings on your calendar while
you focus on closing and delivering.

A founding insurance broker rated one of our outreach emails
9.5/10. He said it was the first cold outreach that sounded like
a peer, not a vendor. That's the bar I'm aiming for.

Founding pricing locked for 12 months for the first 20 clients
per vertical:

- Insurance brokers + consulting: $1,997/mo CAD
- Construction, real estate, dental: $1,497/mo CAD
- Moving + cleaning (waiting list): $997/mo CAD

First-30-day guarantee on every vertical: miss the target, next
month is free.

If you're an operator who's tired of being the sales department,
this is for you. Book a 20-minute demo: crystallux.org

If you know someone who'd benefit, tag them or forward — I'd
rather land leads through my network than any other channel.

Canadian-built. CASL-compliant. Shipping now.

— Mary
```

### Engagement strategy for the post

- Pin the post to Mary's profile for 30 days
- Reply to every comment within 2 hours during launch day
- DM anyone who comments with a specific question — thank them + offer the Calendly link
- Do NOT respond with the same template to every comment — LinkedIn's algorithm penalises generic replies

**Do not pitch:** for the first 30 days, press is lower-priority than founder-network + LinkedIn. Pitch press only after the first 3-5 paying clients are signed and a real case study exists. Press without a case study becomes a launch-party article, not a real-business article.

## First 10 people to tag for amplification

Launch day is a coordinated effort. Approach these 10 specific people **before launch** (email 7-14 days ahead) with the soft ask: "I'm launching on [date]. Would you comment on or share my LinkedIn post that day?"

Candidate profile for the 10:

- Mary's **direct mentors or advisors** — 2-3 people
- Early **founding-client** relationships (with consent) — 1-2 people
- Canadian **founder peers** in related SaaS — 2-3 people
- **LinkedIn-active Canadian SMB operators** Mary has relationships with — 2-3 people
- **Domain experts** (Canadian B2B sales, Canadian marketing) with public LinkedIn presence — 1-2 people

Do NOT ask:
- People Mary doesn't have a real relationship with (feels transactional)
- People who have stated "I don't engage on LinkedIn launch posts"
- Competitors (don't tip them off, don't invite awkwardness)

## Metrics to track on launch day + week 1

- **LinkedIn post impressions** (personal + company)
- **LinkedIn post engagements** (comments, shares, reactions) — especially signal from ICP-matching accounts
- **LinkedIn profile views** on Mary's account
- **Inbound DM count**
- **Email network responses** — opens, replies, forwards
- **Calendly bookings attributable to launch-day traffic**
- **Crystallux.org traffic** (Plausible or equivalent privacy-friendly analytics)
- **Company page followers gained**
- **Inbound enquiries captured in HubSpot**

Day 1 benchmarks (realistic):

- LinkedIn impressions: 5,000-20,000 (personal post); 500-2,000 (company post)
- Engagements: 30-100+ on personal; 10-50 on company
- Calendly bookings: 5-15 (if network activation works well)
- Signed clients within 72 hours: 1-3 (early adopters who were waiting)

Week 1 total signed: 3-5 new clients is a strong launch; 1-2 is on-par; zero means the network activation didn't work and month 1 needs pure outbound volume to compensate.
