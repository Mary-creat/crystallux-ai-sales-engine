# Proof of Concept (POC) Guide — First 5 Clients

**Purpose:** specific POC offer for the first 5 founding clients per vertical. Converts the "I'd need to see this work before paying full price" objection into a concrete 14-day paid trial.

**Who this is for:** prospects who are qualified and interested but deferring the commitment. The POC reduces the commitment threshold while keeping the client economically aligned with us.

**Who this is NOT for:** tire-kickers, clients asking for a free trial, clients who've already been through a demo and are ready to sign. Founding pricing is the default — POC is the exception.

---

## The offer

**14-day Proof of Concept at 50% off the founding rate.**

| Vertical | Founding rate | POC price (50% off 14-day prorate) |
|---|---|---|
| Moving / Cleaning | $997/mo | $230 flat fee for 14 days |
| Construction / Real Estate / Dental | $1,497/mo | $350 flat fee for 14 days |
| Insurance / Consulting | $1,997/mo | $465 flat fee for 14 days |

Flat-fee pricing (not per-day) so accounting is simple: one invoice, one payment, one clear period.

---

## What the client gets during POC

- **Full platform access** — dashboard, workflows, every feature. No crippled mode.
- **Reduced scale** — roughly 40-50% of the founding-tier monthly target:
  - Consulting: 10 qualified calls target (vs 20 at full tier)
  - Insurance: 10 qualified meetings target (vs 20)
  - Real Estate: 6 listing appointments target (vs 15)
  - Construction: 10 qualified leads target (vs 20)
  - Dental: 15 qualified consults target (vs 30)
  - Moving/Cleaning: 15 qualified leads target (vs 30/25)
- **One outreach channel activated** — email by default (the lowest-friction channel). Additional channels ($150 add-on) if the client wants to validate multi-channel behavior.
- **One onboarding call** — 30 minutes, same as a full-paid client
- **One mid-POC check-in call** — 15 minutes on day 7 to review results and address friction
- **One final debrief** — 30 minutes on day 14 to review outcomes and pitch conversion

---

## What the client does NOT get during POC

- **Video outreach (Tavus)** — requires 24h replica training + paid Tavus account, disproportionate overhead for 14 days
- **Voice outreach (Vapi)** — DNCL compliance work is the same for POC as for full, not cost-effective for 14 days
- **Dedicated success manager** — only available on Intelligence tier
- **Priority 4h support** — POCs use standard 24h response window
- **Custom CRM integration** — add-on available but at full one-time fee

## POC conversion pitch (day 14 debrief)

30-minute call, 4-slot structure:

### 1. Recap the numbers (10 min)

Show the client's dashboard. Concrete numbers:
- Prospects surfaced
- Outreach sent
- Replies received
- Qualified meetings booked
- Vs the POC target

If target hit: "You've hit your target. The founding rate is locked now if you convert. Here's the contract for $[full rate]/month — 12 months at founding pricing."

If target missed by <20%: "We're close. What's missing is [specific gap]. I'd propose extending the POC by 7 days for free to finish the validation, then convert to founding rate."

If target missed by >20%: "We're not on track for your target. The question I have to ask honestly is: is this a fit? If yes, what changed between signing the POC and now? If no, I respect that."

### 2. Address friction (10 min)

One direct question: "What would make you hesitate to sign the founding contract right now?"

Listen. Don't defend. If the friction is real (pricing, channel mix, compliance concern), address it directly. If the friction is vague ("let me think about it"), push gently: "What specifically do you want to think about? Is it the price, the outcome, or the fit?"

### 3. Present founding conversion terms (5 min)

- Founding rate locked for 12 months
- Full-scale monthly target (the POC reduced target doubles)
- All channels unlocked
- Guarantee applies: 10-meetings / 5-appointments / 15-consults in first 30 paid days or next month free
- No setup fee, no upgrade fee

### 4. Close (5 min)

"Two paths:

Path one: we convert today. I send the founding contract in the next hour. Your card is charged $[full rate] starting [date]. The POC period counts toward your first 30 days for the guarantee.

Path two: you need more time. I don't want to pressure — I want to make sure this is right for you. What do you need to decide, and by when?"

Capture the answer verbatim. If "I need to check with my partner", set a specific follow-up date. If "I need to think", offer 48 hours with a scheduled follow-up call.

## Testimonial collection during POC

The POC is the best window for strong testimonials because the client is engaged and seeing results in real time.

**Day 7 ask (during mid-POC check-in call):**

> "Quick question at the end of today's call — when this POC converts, would you be open to a 10-minute interview for a short case study? Either named or anonymised, up to you."

**Day 14 ask (during debrief):**

> "Last thing — the results we covered today are good enough to be a case study. Want me to draft one? You'd review every word before anything goes public."

If the client is enthusiastic: schedule the interview for day 16-18. Use the script in `docs/commercial/TESTIMONIAL_COLLECTION.md`.

If the client is hesitant: don't push. Offer a shorter quote ("a one-liner, still your words, anonymised if you prefer"). Any proof is better than no proof.

Review with lawyer before adopting.

## Success metrics for the POC program

Track these on a rolling basis across all POCs:

- **POC-to-founding conversion rate** — target ≥70%. If below, the POC offer is too loose (we're converting unqualified prospects) or the target is unrealistic.
- **POC completion rate** — target ≥95%. A cancelled POC signals onboarding friction or platform bugs.
- **Days from POC sign to founding conversion** — target ≤16 (14 POC + 2 decision).
- **Testimonial capture rate from POC clients** — target ≥50%. POCs that convert usually produce strong quotes; we should be capturing most of them.

If conversion rate drops below 60% for 3 consecutive POCs, the POC terms are under-delivering — review copy, channel mix, and targeting before launching the next one.

## When NOT to offer a POC

The POC is a conversion tool, not a default offer. Withhold it when:

- **The prospect hasn't done a demo yet.** Demo first, then POC only if they're pushing back on full commitment.
- **The prospect is asking for "a few weeks to test it free".** That's a free-trial ask, not a POC. Politely decline; offer the 14-day free trial on the standard subscription instead (unpaid, but requires a card on file).
- **You're the 3rd vendor in a procurement process at a larger org.** The POC won't win procurement — a full contract with a pilot-phase clause is the right move.
- **The prospect's vertical is inactive** (legal, moving, cleaning currently). Don't offer POCs for verticals we haven't operationally validated.

If the above trigger, the right response is: "Let me set you up with a 14-day free trial instead — no charge, cancel any time. I want to make sure you see the full thing work before anyone's card gets charged."
