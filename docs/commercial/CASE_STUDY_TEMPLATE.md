# Case Study Template

**Purpose:** reusable template for publishing completed-client wins on crystallux.org/case-studies, in outbound emails, and on LinkedIn. Fill in every bracketed field per client.

**Usage policy:** every case study requires signed consent from the client before publication (see `docs/commercial/TESTIMONIAL_COLLECTION.md` → Usage Rights Agreement). Anonymised studies are acceptable when consent for named attribution isn't possible.

**Template:**

**Template:**

[Client contact name/title] said: "[direct quote about the pain]". They'd tried [previous solutions — e.g., "an SDR agency for $4,500/month" OR "three different LinkedIn automation tools"], and each either [specific failure — e.g., "sent outreach that felt robotic" OR "delivered unqualified leads that wasted broker time"].

## The solution

What Crystallux did, in order. Keep to 4-6 short paragraphs. Reference specific features or workflows (e.g., "Campaign Router v2", "Apollo enrichment", "Tavus video outreach") when relevant — this builds credibility with technical buyers.

**Template:**

### 1. Vertical-tuned onboarding

In the first 48 hours post-contract, we configured [Client Name]'s workspace for the [vertical] vertical with [specific channel mix — e.g., "email + LinkedIn + voice" OR "email + SMS + video"]. Their [compliance consideration — e.g., "RIBO licensing context" OR "RCDSO advertising rules"] informed every outreach template.

### 2. ICP definition + prospect sourcing

Apollo + [additional sources — e.g., "RIBO directory + LinkedIn Sales Navigator"] surfaced [N] matching prospects in their service area ([geography]). Lead Research v2 ran Claude-authored research on each, producing a one-paragraph summary + the most relevant pain signal for each prospect.

### 3. Outreach at scale

Within seven days, Claude-authored outreach sent across [channel list]. Every email was tuned to the specific lead's research: [example pain-signal angle — e.g., "renewal-shock messaging for homeowners whose auto insurance just jumped 12%" OR "M&A-induced policy gaps for commercial clients"]. The client approved the first batch, then the system ran autonomously.

### 4. Reply handling + booking

Qualified replies flowed through the reply-ingestion workflow into [Client Name]'s calendar. [Client Name]'s team stepped in only at the conversation stage — no time spent on copy, cadence, or follow-up timing.

### 5. Weekly optimisation

Our weekly 15-minute check-in surfaced [specific optimisation — e.g., "a 22% reply-rate lift after we shifted subject lines from question-form to statement-form"]. Within a month, the pipeline reliably produced [outcome volume].

## Visual suggestions

For the designer laying this into the web template:

- **Hero image:** client logo (with permission) OR a clean anonymised icon. No stock photography.
- **Dashboard screenshot:** anonymised (blur client names, randomise lead counts). Place below the "Solution" section.
- **Before/after bar chart:** reply rate + meetings per month. Two grouped bars per metric.
- **Timeline visual:** horizontal 4-week strip with milestone markers. Clean icons only.
- **Quote callout:** pulled from body into a large-type panel with client photo (if consent for named attribution) or vertical icon (if anonymised).

## Consent + review workflow

Before publishing any named case study:

1. Collect written consent via the Usage Rights Agreement in `docs/commercial/TESTIMONIAL_COLLECTION.md`.
2. Draft the case study using this template.
3. Send the full draft to the client for review.
4. Incorporate their edits (typically 1-2 rounds).
5. Confirm final sign-off in writing (email is acceptable).
6. Publish.

Anonymised case studies don't require consent for attribution — but must not be reverse-identifiable (avoid exact revenue figures, client counts, or geographic specificity that narrows the field to one company).
