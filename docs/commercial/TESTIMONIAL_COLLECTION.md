# Testimonial Collection Process

**Purpose:** reliable, PIPEDA-compliant process for collecting social proof (quotes, logos, case studies) from Crystallux clients. Every testimonial requires written consent before use.

---

## When to ask

Timing matters more than the ask. Three natural windows:

1. **Day 14 of service** — after the first major win (first meeting booked, first client closed from Crystallux pipeline). Client is riding the high of early proof.
2. **End of first paid month** — after the 30-day guarantee has been hit (or exceeded). Numbers are in.
3. **Month 3 retention** — strongest signal of real value. Quotes from month 3+ clients close the most deals.

**Don't ask** in week 1 (too early; no proof), during a service incident, or while a billing dispute is open.

## Interview script — 15-minute call

If they book the call instead of replying by email, use this script. **Goal:** extract 6-8 natural quotes that can be lightly edited for use.

### Opening (2 minutes)

> "Thanks for the time. Three quick reminders before we start: I'll record this call unless you prefer not to. I'll send you the edited quote back before anything gets published. You can revise or pull anything at any point."

Confirm recording consent. Start recording.

### Questions (11 minutes)

1. **What was your pipeline like before Crystallux?** *(anchor the before-state, 2 min)*
2. **What made you finally decide to sign?** *(uncover the trigger, 1 min)*
3. **What was the first thing that worked?** *(fishes for a specific moment — "the first email that actually got a reply", etc., 2 min)*
4. **What would you tell another [broker/contractor/agent] who's considering Crystallux?** *(direct-quote bait, 2 min)*
5. **What specific number or result captures the impact?** *(forces quantification, 2 min)*
6. **What's been the biggest surprise — positive or negative?** *(pulls honesty, 2 min)*

### Close (2 minutes)

> "Last thing — are you comfortable with me attributing a quote to you by name, or would you prefer we publish this anonymised with just 'Broker in Toronto' or similar?"

Confirm preference. Confirm you'll send the cleaned-up quote + usage request within 3 business days.

### Post-call

- Stop recording. Transcribe within 24 hours (Otter.ai or Descript).
- Extract 6-8 candidate quotes (30-60 words each).
- Apply the polishing process below.
- Email back: cleaned quote + consent form within 3 business days.

## Quote polishing process

Raw interview transcripts need light cleanup before use. Rules:

### What to cut

- Filler words: "um", "like", "you know", "so basically"
- Repetitions: "the pipeline, the pipeline, what I mean is the pipeline"
- Backtracks: "actually, wait, let me start over"
- Negatives that don't serve the quote: "I mean, it's not perfect, but..."

### What to keep

- The specific number or observation that makes the quote concrete.
- The client's natural voice — don't over-polish into marketing-speak.
- Direct cause-and-effect: "when X happened, we saw Y".

### What to flag back to the client

- Any edit that changes meaning — always confirm.
- Any addition of words not in the original — the client must approve verbatim.
- Shortening for pull-quote use — confirm the short version preserves their meaning.

### Example transformation

**Raw transcript:**

> "So, um, what really surprised me was, like, the fact that, you know, we started seeing replies in week one. I mean, we've tried, like, three different LinkedIn tools before and they all felt super robotic. But this one, it actually sounded like, you know, an advisor was reaching out. And we booked — I think it was 11 meetings in the first month? Yeah, 11."

**Cleaned:**

> "We started seeing replies in week one. We'd tried three different LinkedIn tools before — they all felt robotic. This one actually sounded like an advisor was reaching out. We booked 11 meetings in the first month."

**Pull-quote (landing page, 15 words):**

> "It actually sounded like an advisor was reaching out, not a tool."

Send all three back to client for approval.

## Where to use each type of quote

| Quote length | Best placement |
|---|---|
| **Pull-quote (10-15 words)** | Landing-page testimonial tile, pricing-page proof block, sales one-pager, email subject lines |
| **Short quote (20-40 words)** | LinkedIn post, case-study pull-quote, social ads |
| **Medium quote (40-80 words)** | Case-study body, email outreach Sequence A email 2, post-demo email attachment |
| **Long quote (80-150 words)** | Full case-study intro paragraph, founder-voice post referencing a client, PR pitch |
| **Logo only (no quote)** | "Featured clients" logo row, pricing page, landing page trust row |

Always ask permission for the longer quote first; the shorter versions derive from the approved long version.

## Storage + record-keeping

Keep a master spreadsheet (Google Sheet or Notion DB) with:

- Client name, vertical, contract date
- Testimonial collected date
- Interview recording URL (Loom / Otter.ai), password-protected
- Raw transcript
- Cleaned quote (all lengths)
- Consent form (PDF), date signed
- Published channels + date published
- Revocation status (if any)

Retention: 7 years or until the client formally revokes consent, whichever is earlier. Stored in the Crystallux Google Drive under `/legal/testimonials/[client-name]/`.

---

## Quarterly refresh

Every quarter, revisit the master spreadsheet:

- Which quotes are getting used? Which are collecting dust?
- Are any clients overdue for a month-6 or month-12 update quote? (Retention milestones often produce the strongest proof.)
- Any clients churned whose quote should be retired?
- Any quotes feeling stale against current pricing / channel mix?

Refresh the live library: retire stale, promote new, update landing-page rotations.
