What it does

CIRO is the layer that turns raw activity into things you should know about. Every message, every booking, every conflict, every spike — all surfaced when it matters, not buried in dashboards.

📜

Unified comms log

Every email + SMS + WhatsApp + voice + LinkedIn message in one chronological feed. When a customer asks "did you contact me," you have the answer in two clicks.

🔥

Hot lead alerts

Lead score crosses 80 with intent signals? CIRO alerts you immediately. The lead gets escalated before they go cold.

📆

Calendar conflict detection

Overlapping bookings, tight gaps between meetings, double-booked rooms — flagged automatically before the customer notices.

📨

Daily briefing

Morning email summarizing yesterday: new leads, conversions, comms volume, alerts cleared. Read it while drinking coffee.

💼

LinkedIn auto-DM

When LinkedIn API access lands, CIRO sends connection requests + first messages to qualified leads. Approval gates available.

🚨

Alert center

All CIRO-generated events (hot leads, conflicts, anomalies) feed one alerts page. Acknowledge or dismiss with a click.

How it works

CIRO runs in the background. You configure alert thresholds once and check the briefing each morning.

Configure thresholds

Set the lead score that triggers a hot-lead alert. Set how-close-is-too-close for calendar gaps. One-time setup.

CIRO observes

Workflows run continuously: comms log aggregator, hot lead detector, calendar conflict detector, daily briefing generator.

Alerts surface

High-priority alerts (hot lead, calendar conflict) hit you in real time. Lower-priority items land in the briefing.

You act

Acknowledge from the alert center. CIRO learns from your acknowledgment patterns and stops surfacing what you ignore.
