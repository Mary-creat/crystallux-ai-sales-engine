About

Built in Toronto for Canadian operators.

Crystallux was started by Mary Akintunde to solve the same pipeline problem she watched her network struggle with year after year: the sales engine only moves when you personally move it.

The founder

Mary Akintunde is a Canadian licensed insurance professional based in Toronto. She spent years watching fellow operators — brokers, contractors, consultants, dentists — burn their weekends on cold outreach and still hit month-long dry spells. The outreach tools they tried were templated, sounded like marketing departments, and converted at 1–3%. The SDR agencies they hired charged $4,500/month and fired their proposals at the same ICP with no niche tuning.

Mary built Crystallux to be the thing she wished existed: a universal sales engine that reads like a peer in your trade, handles the end-to-end pipeline, and ships with a money-back guarantee so operators aren't the ones eating the risk.

The thesis

Every Canadian SMB operator we've talked to has the same complaint: the pipeline only moves when you personally move it. Weekends disappear into proposals. Bizdev eats the hours that should go to delivery. And the outreach that actually works — peer-to-peer, specific, human — doesn't scale past 10 leads a week.

Claude can write peer-advisor outreach better than any templated tool. Combine that with Apollo sourcing, multi-channel delivery, and per-vertical overlays, and you get a system that replaces the SDR agency and the in-house BDR at a fraction of either cost.

A founding insurance broker rated our cold outreach 9.5/10 — said it was the first cold outreach that sounded like a peer, not a vendor. That's the bar.

Canadian by design

  • Based in Toronto — incorporated in Ontario, physical business address, Canadian bank
  • CASL-compliant outreach — every message carries sender identification, business address, and working unsubscribe
  • PIPEDA-aligned data handling — Canadian privacy law governs how we handle client + lead data
  • Stripe-billed in CAD — plus GST/HST auto-calculated per province
  • Vertical compliance review — RIBO for insurance, RCDSO for dental, RECO for real estate, LSO for legal (when activated)
  • DNCL compliance — voice outreach gated on a real CRTC lookup before activation

The platform

Crystallux runs on a modern Canadian stack: Supabase for database + row-level security, n8n for workflow orchestration, Claude (Anthropic) for outreach generation, Apollo for prospect enrichment, Stripe for billing. Dashboard built on the same codebase — single shell serving admin, client, and future ops roles with enforced client isolation.

The engine is universal. The overlays are vertical-specific. The infrastructure is production-grade. The team is small and relentless on unit economics.
