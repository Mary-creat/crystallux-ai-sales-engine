Pain signals we target

The brokers we work with run 3 to 20 person shops competing against big-brand brokers with marketing departments. Crystallux speaks to the specific pressures of the Canadian insurance market:

  • Renewal-season window too narrow for manual outreach.
  • Rate-shopping sites eating client retention.
  • Commercial broker-of-record switches taking 4 to 6 months.
  • Claims-denial fears post-wildfire or post-flood.
  • Coverage gaps from M and A activity in client portfolios.
  • Rate compression on personal auto lines.
  • Losing quotes to aggregators on price.
  • Referral pipeline flattening year over year.

Compliance-aware from day one

Every outreach template is designed for Canadian insurance advertising rules. CASL footer with sender identification, business address, working unsubscribe. RIBO-aware messaging: no outcome claims, no comparative advertising against named competitors, appropriate licensing disclosure on commercial-line outreach. DNCL compliance gate on voice outreach.

We do not activate voice outreach to Canadian numbers until a real CRTC DNCL lookup replaces the placeholder. Non-negotiable.
