How Crystallux Insurance Network built Canada's first AI-native MGA

A founder-led MGA proving the Crystallux platform from the inside.

Background

Crystallux Insurance Network is the first MGA operating fully on the Crystallux platform. Founder Mary built it from a solo position: no team of operations engineers, no legacy carrier-portal sprawl, no manual spreadsheet workflow stack. The thesis: a modern MGA in 2026 should be built around AI agents, not around a head-count plan.

Challenge

Traditional MGA infrastructure assumes a back-office team of 5–15 people: licensing manager, compliance officer, commission specialist, suitability reviewer, follow-up clerk. Each carries operational cost and operational risk. Mary needed to run those functions with one principal + a small set of AI agents that could pre-screen, schedule, and surface — leaving the human attention for the judgment calls.

Solution

Crystallux's AI Compliance Engine pre-screens every application. Behavioral Intelligence triggers reviews + outreach. Production Reporting gives every carrier appointment a live view. Carrier Integration Foundation lets the MGA place with digital-first carriers (PolicyMe, Walnut, Intact) on day one. The Pre-Meeting Briefing AI walks the advisor into every meeting prepared. Training Coach + Onboarding Curriculum get new sub-agents production-ready in 30 days.

Results

Results will be filled in as Crystallux Insurance Network's production data accumulates. The platform is the proof: 60+ Layer 2 workflows, 30+ universal workflows, full audit trail on every action, real-time dashboards across advisor, principal, and insurer.

"The Crystallux platform lets one principal run an MGA at the operational quality of a 15-person back office. That's the unlock. It compresses the timeline from 'have an MGA license' to 'placing business' from 18 months to 60 days." — Mary, Founder
