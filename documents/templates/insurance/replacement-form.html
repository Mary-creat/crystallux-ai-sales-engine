Critical notice — do NOT cancel existing coverage until the new policy has been issued and is in force. Cancelling early can leave you uninsured and may forfeit accumulated cash value, contestability protection, or preferred underwriting class earned over time.

Considerations before replacement

  • Underwriting reset. The new policy starts a new 2-year contestability period and a new suicide-clause period.
  • Health status. If your health has changed since the existing policy was issued, the new policy may have higher premiums or be declined entirely.
  • Cash value. Whole-life replacement may forfeit accumulated cash value or trigger taxable disposition.
  • Surrender charges. Some existing policies impose charges if cancelled before a defined period.
